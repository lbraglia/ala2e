
Data on Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia (BPD) from 223 low birth weight 
infants (weighing less than 1750 grams).


Source: Data courtesy of Dr. Linda Van Marter.

Reference: Van Marter, L.J., Leviton, A., Kuban, K.C.K., Pagano, M. and Allred, E.N. (1990).  
Maternal glucocorticoid therapy and reduced risk of bronchopulmonary dysplasia.
Pediatrics, 86, 331-336.


Description:

The data are from a study of low birth weight infants in a neonatal intensive 
care unit. The study was designed to examine the development of bronchopulmonary 
dysplasia (BPD), a chronic lung disease, in a sample of 223 infants weighing 
less than 1750 grams. The response variable is binary, denoting whether an 
infant develops BPD by day 28 of life (where BPD is defined by both oxygen 
requirement and compatible chest radiograph).


Variable List: 

BPD (0,1), Birth Weight (grams), Gestational Age (weeks), Toxemia (0,1).
